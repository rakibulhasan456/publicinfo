DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
The Department of Justice (DOJ) serves as the primary legal authority across the state, overseeing the enforcement of laws, regulations, and legal procedures. Its role is comprehensive, ensuring justice and legal compliance for citizens, businesses, and government institutions alike.

DOJ works closely with the State, Business, and Company, providing essential legal support and oversight.

For civilians, DOJ handles various cases, ensures lawful proceedings, and legal consultation and documentation.

For businesses and companies, DOJ is integral in managing legal paperwork, verifying compliance, and providing official legal documentation necessary for operations. Every business seeking a lease or official registration requires legal review and assistance from DOJ lawyers, who ensure that all contractual and regulatory obligations are met.

Through its wide-ranging authority, the Department of Justice upholds the rule of law, safeguards citizens’ rights, and maintains legal integrity in all aspects of state governance and public affairs.
DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE (DOJ) SERVICES & FEES
The Department of Justice (DOJ) provides legal assistance, court representation, and document services to ensure fairness and justice for all citizens. Below is a simple explanation of our services and the fees that may apply.
Service Name Description Fee
Civil Disputes If you are involved in a legal dispute with another civilian (such as money, property, or contract issues), the DOJ can represent the case in court. Civilian Pays: $2,000
PD/EMS Disputes For cases involving the Government Departments, the DOJ ensures the matter is handled lawfully and fairly. Fee is negotiable
(Govt. Employees are not required to pay)
Business-Related Legal Services The DOJ reviews business applications and provides legal support for business approvals. If Business is Approved: 15% of the business value

If Business is Not Approved: $3,500
Legal Consultation Citizens may request legal advice to understand their rights and legal options before proceeding. Negotiable with the client
Legal Document Services The DOJ prepares official legal documents to ensure they are valid and lawful. Contract Drafting: $1,000

FCS Drafting: $2,000
Long-Term Legal Representation For ongoing or complex legal matters, citizens may hire a DOJ attorney long-term. Negotiable with the client
Vehicle Impound Release Fees If the same vehicle is impounded multiple times, the release cost increases. First Release: $5,000

Second Release: +$3,000

Third Release: +$3,000
Suspended Driver’s License A driver’s license is suspended at 15 points. Base fee: $3,000

Additional fee: $300 per point over 15
Criminal Record Removal Criminal record removal is decided by the District Attorney’s Office and is not guaranteed. Fees based on MDT points:

0 points: $20,000
1–15 points: $30,000
16–30 points: $50,000
31–50 points: $100,000
51–100 points: $200,000

More than 100 points: Not eligible
