DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE
The Department of Justice (DOJ) serves as the primary legal authority across the state, overseeing the enforcement of laws, regulations, and legal procedures. Its role is comprehensive, ensuring justice and legal compliance for citizens, businesses, and government institutions alike.

DOJ works closely with the State, Business, Company, and DLRA, providing essential legal support and oversight.

For civilians, DOJ handles various cases, ensures lawful proceedings, and issues No Objection Certificates (NOC) required for obtaining licenses such as hunting, mining, and weapon permits.

For businesses and companies, DOJ is integral in managing legal paperwork, verifying compliance, and providing official legal documentation necessary for operations. Every business seeking a lease or official registration requires legal review and assistance from DOJ lawyers, who ensure that all contractual and regulatory obligations are met.

Through its wide-ranging authority, the Department of Justice upholds the rule of law, safeguards citizens’ rights, and maintains legal integrity in all aspects of state governance and public affairs.
